Percy Jackson Book Series

Percy Jackson & the Olympians

The Original Series (2005-2009)

The series that started it all follows Percy Jackson, a young demigod who discovers he's a son of Poseidon. Through five action-packed books, Percy and his friends face prophecies, monsters, and ultimately the rise of the Titan lord Kronos. The series masterfully weaves Greek mythology into the modern world, following Percy from age 12 to 16 as he fights to save Olympus while dealing with both godly and mortal challenges.

Books in the Series:

  1. The Lightning Thief (2005)
  2. The Sea of Monsters (2006)
  3. The Titan's Curse (2007)
  4. The Battle of the Labyrinth (2008)
  5. The Last Olympian (2009)

The Heroes of Olympus

The Epic Follow-up Series (2010-2014)

This series expands the universe by introducing Roman demigods and their camp, Camp Jupiter. Following seven heroes from both Greek and Roman camps, the series tackles the awakening of Gaea and the giants. New characters like Jason Grace, Piper McLean, and Leo Valdez join familiar faces in a quest that spans both the United States and ancient lands, bringing together two long-separated aspects of the mythological world.

Books in the Series:

  1. The Lost Hero (2010)
  2. The Son of Neptune (2011)
  3. The Mark of Athena (2012)
  4. The House of Hades (2013)
  5. The Blood of Olympus (2014)

The Trials of Apollo

The Divine Punishment Series (2016-2020)

After angering Zeus, the god Apollo is cast down to Earth as a mortal teenager. Now known as Lester Papadopoulos, he must restore five ancient Oracles and face a new threat to both the mortal and godly worlds. The series offers a unique perspective as it's narrated by a former god experiencing human life, while also providing closure to many character arcs from the previous series. Through his trials, Apollo learns about humanity, humility, and heroism.

Books in the Series:

  1. The Hidden Oracle (2016)
  2. The Dark Prophecy (2017)
  3. The Burning Maze (2018)
  4. The Tyrant's Tomb (2019)
  5. The Tower of Nero (2020)
