The Twelve Olympians

The Twelve Olympians are the principal deities of the Greek pantheon, residing in Mount Olympus under the leadership of Zeus. In the Percy Jackson universe, they've relocated to America, with Mount Olympus now situated above the Empire State Building in New York City.

The Big Three

The most powerful of the Olympian gods are the three brothers who divided the world between them after overthrowing the Titans.

Zeus

King of the Gods • Lord of the Sky

Zeus is the king of the Olympian gods and god of the sky, weather, law, order, and justice. He wields the master bolt, the most powerful weapon in the universe. After the defeat of their father Kronos, Zeus and his brothers drew lots to decide their domains, with Zeus winning the sky and leadership of the gods.

  • Symbol: Lightning Bolt, Eagle
  • Domain: Sky, Thunder, Law
  • Notable Children: Thalia Grace, Jason Grace

Poseidon

Lord of the Seas • Earthshaker

Poseidon is the god of the sea, earthquakes, storms, and horses. He rules the ocean and all its creatures, wielding his powerful trident. Though often temperamental like the sea itself, Poseidon is generally more reasonable than his brothers and shows great affection for his children, including Percy Jackson.

  • Symbol: Trident, Horse
  • Domain: Sea, Earthquakes, Horses
  • Notable Children: Percy Jackson, Tyson

Hades

Lord of the Dead • The Rich One

Though not technically an Olympian, Hades is equal in power to his brothers. He rules the Underworld and all its riches, maintaining order in the realm of the dead. Often misunderstood, Hades is more reserved and strict rather than evil, maintaining crucial balance in the world.

  • Symbol: Helm of Darkness, Cerberus
  • Domain: Underworld, Wealth, Death
  • Notable Children: Nico di Angelo, Bianca di Angelo

The Other Olympians

Athena

Goddess of Wisdom and War Strategy

Born from Zeus's head, Athena is the goddess of wisdom, war strategy, and useful arts. Her children are born from her thoughts, inheriting her wisdom and strategic mind.

  • Symbol: Owl, Olive Tree
  • Domain: Wisdom, Strategy, Crafts
  • Notable Children: Annabeth Chase

Ares

God of War

Ares represents the violent and untamed aspects of war. Unlike Athena, who represents military strategy and wisdom, Ares is about the brutal nature of battle.

  • Symbol: Spear, Boar
  • Domain: War, Violence, Bloodshed
  • Notable Children: Clarisse La Rue

Apollo

God of Music, Poetry, and Prophecy

Apollo is the god of many things including music, poetry, art, oracles, archery, medicine, sun, light and knowledge. He's known for his wit and charm.

  • Symbol: Lyre, Bow
  • Domain: Music, Prophecy, Healing
  • Notable Children: Will Solace

Artemis

Goddess of the Hunt and Moon

Apollo's twin sister, Artemis is the goddess of the hunt, moon, archery, and wildlife. She leads the Hunters of Artemis, a group of immortal maidens.

  • Symbol: Bow, Deer
  • Domain: Hunt, Moon, Wildlife
  • Followers: The Hunters of Artemis

Hephaestus

God of Fire and Forge

Master craftsman of Olympus, Hephaestus is the god of fire, metalworking, and craftsmanship. He creates the gods' weapons and magical items.

  • Symbol: Hammer, Anvil
  • Domain: Fire, Forge, Crafts
  • Notable Children: Charles Beckendorf

Aphrodite

Goddess of Love and Beauty

Born from sea foam, Aphrodite is the goddess of love, beauty, and attraction. Her appearance changes based on one's perception of beauty.

  • Symbol: Dove, Rose
  • Domain: Love, Beauty, Passion
  • Notable Children: Piper McLean

Hermes

God of Travelers and Merchants

The messenger of the gods, Hermes is also the god of commerce, thieves, travelers, sports, and border crossings. He guides souls to the Underworld.

  • Symbol: Caduceus, Winged Sandals
  • Domain: Travel, Commerce, Communication
  • Notable Children: Luke Castellan

Dionysus

God of Wine and Festivities

Known as Mr. D at Camp Half-Blood, Dionysus serves as the camp director as punishment from Zeus. He's the god of wine, parties, and madness.

  • Symbol: Grape Vine, Leopard
  • Domain: Wine, Festivals, Ecstasy
  • Role: Camp Half-Blood Director

Demeter

Goddess of Agriculture

Demeter is the goddess of agriculture, fertility, and the harvest. She's responsible for the seasons and the growth of all plants.

  • Symbol: Wheat, Torch
  • Domain: Agriculture, Harvest, Seasons
  • Notable Children: Katie Gardner
